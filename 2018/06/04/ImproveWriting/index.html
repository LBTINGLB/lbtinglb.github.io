  • Response to Microsoft acquring Coding Site GitHub
    ``
    So hell has frozen over. I read the rumors yesterday and the confirmation this morning before you posted it but had to re-check from as many sources as I could before I really believed it.

In all seriousness though, I think this is a very interesting case of coming full circle on “if you can’t beat ‘em, join ‘em”. There’s certainly a bitter taste in the OSS world leftover from the Steve Balmer years of treating Linux as a “cancer” and all open source as a bunch of communist IP thieves. And of course there’s the perception of the “Embrace, Extend, Extinguish” business strategy, but I would really encourage people to try to look past that. In the past several years MS has made a dramatic 180 turn to figure out how to embrace the community and benefit as a business from it rather than try to fight it. Sure, their flagship software products are closed source, but really that’s fine. I do believe the OSS community can benefit as a whole from a lot of the efforts and resources MS pours into it now, but it will continue to be very difficult to shake the reputation they’ve built in the past and to shift the social perception of their OSS efforts to something we can all benefit from rather than continue to view them as a wolf in sheep’s clothing.

I’m not saying they’re a benevolent player in all of this with altruistic motives. Of course not. They are a business and their goal is to make lots and lots of money, often through power and influence. However, it seems they may be coming around the idea that there is indeed lots of money to be made by embracing and fostering the OSS community. We’ll have to see how this all plays out though.
``
